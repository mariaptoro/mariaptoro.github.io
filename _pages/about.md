<p>
  Hi there, I’m Maria Paula Toro-Gómez, a Biologist, from the University of Quindío (UQ), in Colombia. 
  </p>

  I am interested in documenting the evolutionary and ecological aspects, as well as the natural history of amphibians 🐸, particularly from a behavioral perspective. I am part of the Evolution, Ecology and Conservation Research Group (EECO) of the University of Quindío, under the direction of Prof. Fernando Vargas-Salinas. During my time there, I have been able to develop many essential skills in scientific research, including writing scientific papers, sampling methods in amphibians and reptiles, sample preparation, and various other scientific skills and hobbies related to what we call "frog spying 🥷🏾"

<p>
  📚I work as a junior researcher at the Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation of the Colombian Government. In this position, I am actively involved in projects that meticulously document the impact of the armed conflict on indigenous communities in Colombia, as well as how amphibians respond to the intricate interaction between human activities, conflict, and the natural world. My professional career has been enriched by delving into projects that revolve around the analysis of passive acoustic monitoring recordings. A focal point of my expertise lies in the development of state-of-the-art deep learning models for automatic audio classification. This not only underscores my commitment to ecological research but also highlights the fascinating intersection between technology and its application in understanding our natural environment.
  </p>

<p>
  🔎 I am currently pursuing postgraduate opportunities in the realm of behavioral ecology, specifically focusing on the evolutionary dynamics of communication signals in amphibians. I'd love ❤️ to connect with like-minded people or institutions who share my interest on this field. Feel free to reach out!! I'm excited about potential collaborations and opportunities.
</p>

<p>
  Beyond my research activities, I am an advocate for science outreach and effective communication. I live surrounded by mountains in my small town Pijao, Quindío. I am an ice cream enthusiast 🍦 and chocolate lover!
  </p>
